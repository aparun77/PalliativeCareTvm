Major Palliative Care Initiative in Kerala

The scheme titled Kerala Care Universal Palliative Service Scheme was launched to register volunteers, organisations, patients in need of palliative care across Kerala.

A digital platform “Palliative Care Grid” (under “Kerala Care”) was set up to integrate government institutions, voluntary organisations and home‑care units across the state.

A Government Order (G.O.) was issued (G.O.(Rt)No.2744/2022/H&FWD dated 16‑11‑2022) constituting a high power committee & working group on palliative care under the Health & Family Welfare Department.

Organisation / Implementation Context in Thiruvananthapuram (TVM)

The capital district (Thiruvananthapuram) of Kerala will be covered under the state‑wide scheme (so our “TVM organisation” can be part of this network).

The implementation happens via local self‑government institutions (panchayats/municipalities) delivering primary palliative care, and through home‑care units.

Volunteering, care, registration of patients are key components.

Volunteer Network

Volunteers are trained through short-term courses by NGOs or local bodies.

They act as the bridge between families and healthcare teams.

Their role extends beyond medical care — covering emotional support, social integration, and rehabilitation.

Services: medical care, pain relief, counseling, caregiver support, and provision of assistive devices.

Future Directions

Enhancing tele-palliative care and home-based rehabilitation.

Creating district-level integrated care hubs linking hospitals, community teams, and NGOs.

Building research capacity to measure outcomes and quality of life impacts.

Strengths and Global Recognition

Thiruvananthapuram’s model is cited as an international best practice by WHO and The Lancet Commission on Global Access to Palliative Care (2018).

Integration of state, community, and NGO sectors is a unique feature.

Gaps in service coverage in urban slums and peri-urban areas.
